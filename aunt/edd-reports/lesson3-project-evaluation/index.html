714 · 5.1The construct

Defining the constructWhat an experimental design is

Experimental designA research method that tests cause and effect by changing one thing on purpose and comparing the results against a group that was left unchanged.
In plain terms: try the program on one group, not on another, and see what differs.
714 · 5.1The gold standard

The randomized controlled trial (RCT)

714 · 5.1Building blocks

The heart of the topicThree building blocks

  • RandomizedPeople are placed into groups by chance, removing hidden bias.
  • Control groupA comparison group that does not receive the program.
  • Pre–post measuresMeasure before and after, so change can be seen clearly.
714 · 5.1Trustworthiness

Two kinds of validity

InternalAre we sure the program caused the change — and not something else?
Plain terms: is the result real and not a fluke?
ExternalWould the result hold true elsewhere, with other people or places?
Plain terms: does it generalize beyond this one study?
714 · 5.1Significance

Why this design is the strongest

  • Isolates the causeThe control group rules out other explanations.
  • Removes biasRandom assignment keeps the groups fair and comparable.
  • Trusted evidenceIts results carry the most weight in evaluation.
714 · 5.1Key takeaways

Key takeaways

Purpose
Prove a program truly causes a result.
RCT
Randomize, treat one group, compare to a control.
Validity
Internal = real; external = generalizable.
Strength
The most trusted way to show cause.
