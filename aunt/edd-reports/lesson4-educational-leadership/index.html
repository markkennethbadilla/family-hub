Values & Ethics in Administration

703 · 11.1The construct

Values and ethics, plainly

ValuesThe beliefs we hold about what is important — honesty, fairness, respect, service.
EthicsThose values put into action — doing the right thing, especially when it is hard.
In administration: making fair, honest decisions for the school community.
703 · 11.1Significance

Why values come first in leadership

  • TrustPeople follow leaders they believe are fair and honest.
  • ConsistencyClear values make decisions predictable and just.
  • ExampleA school takes its tone from how its leaders behave.

In practice

Values-Based Leadership

703 · 11.3The construct

Leading from your values

Values-based leadershipA way of leading where decisions and actions flow from clear, shared values rather than from convenience or pressure.
In plain terms: you lead by what you believe is right, and you live it openly.
703 · 11.3In practice

What it looks like day to day

  • Walk the talkLive the values you ask of others.
  • Decide openlyExplain the why, so choices are seen as fair.
  • Empower peopleLead with respect, not fear; build others up.

Governance, plainly

Good governanceRunning an institution in a way that is fair, open, accountable, and serves everyone it is meant to serve.
In plain terms: managing the school honestly and in the open, answerable to the community.
