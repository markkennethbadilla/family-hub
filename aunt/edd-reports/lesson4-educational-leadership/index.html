703 · 11.2The construct

Defining the constructWhat ethical principles are

Ethical principlesThe agreed standards of right conduct that guide how a leader makes decisions and treats people — especially when the choice is difficult.
In plain terms: the rules of "doing right" that a school administrator lives by.
703 · 11.2Rationale

Why principles, not just rules

Rules cannot cover every situation. Principles give a leader a steady way to decide what is right even when no rule fits the case in front of them.
703 · 11.2Principles

The heart of the topicThe core ethical principles

  • Integrity & honestyBe truthful and consistent; keep your word.
  • Fairness & justiceTreat everyone equally; decide without favoritism.
  • Respect for personsHonor the dignity of every student and staff member.
  • AccountabilityOwn your decisions and answer for their results.
703 · 11.2Principles

Two more that guide the work

ConfidentialityProtect private information about students and staff; share it only when it is right to do so.
Service & the common goodPut the welfare of learners and the community above personal interest.
703 · 11.2In practice

A principle in action

A hard choiceA relative applies for a teaching post and is not the most qualified.

The principles of fairness and integrity say: choose the best candidate, openly and on merit — even when it is personally uncomfortable.
703 · 11.2Significance

Why these principles matter

  • TrustPeople trust a leader whose decisions are fair and honest.
  • ConsistencyPrinciples keep decisions steady from one case to the next.
  • Good exampleA school learns ethics from how its leaders behave.
703 · 11.2Key takeaways

Key takeaways

What
Ethical principles are the standards of right conduct.
The core
Integrity, fairness, respect, accountability.
Also
Confidentiality and service to the common good.
Why
They build trust, consistency, and a good example.
