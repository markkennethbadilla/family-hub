HOW TO USE THIS SCRIPT

SLIDE 1 — TITLE
Good morning, everyone. My report covers Chapter 11, Educational Leadership and Ethics — specifically topics 11.1, 11.3, and 11.4. In short, this is about leading schools with integrity: from the values we hold, to how we lead people, to how we govern the institution.


SLIDE 2 — THE THREE TOPICS I COVER
Let me first set the scope. Chapter 11 has several parts, but I cover three: 11.1, values and ethics; 11.3, values-based leadership; and 11.4, good governance. I am not covering 11.2. The thread that ties my three together is simple — from what we believe, to how we lead, to how we govern.


SLIDE 3 — SECTION 11.1
Let us begin with eleven-point-one: values and ethics in administration.


SLIDE 4 — VALUES AND ETHICS, PLAINLY
Two key terms. Values are the beliefs we hold about what is important — honesty, fairness, respect, service. Ethics are those values put into action — doing the right thing, especially when it is hard. In administration, that means making fair, honest decisions for the whole school community.


SLIDE 5 — WHY VALUES COME FIRST IN LEADERSHIP
Why do values come first? Three reasons. Trust — people follow leaders they believe are fair and honest. Consistency — clear values make decisions predictable and just. And example — a school takes its tone from how its leaders behave.


SLIDE 6 — SECTION 11.3
Now to eleven-point-three: values-based leadership.


SLIDE 7 — LEADING FROM YOUR VALUES
Values-based leadership is a way of leading where decisions and actions flow from clear, shared values, rather than from convenience or pressure. In plain terms: you lead by what you believe is right, and you live it openly for others to see.


SLIDE 8 — WHAT IT LOOKS LIKE DAY TO DAY
What does it look like in practice? Walk the talk — live the values you ask of others. Decide openly — explain the why, so choices are seen as fair. And empower people — lead with respect, not fear, and build others up.


SLIDE 9 — SECTION 11.4
Now to eleven-point-four: good governance.


SLIDE 10 — GOVERNANCE, PLAINLY
Good governance means running an institution in a way that is fair, open, accountable, and serves everyone it is meant to serve. In plain terms: managing the school honestly and in the open, and being answerable to the community.


SLIDE 11 — THE PILLARS OF GOOD GOVERNANCE
Good governance rests on four pillars. Transparency — decisions made in the open. Accountability — being answerable for results. Participation — everyone has a voice. And fairness — the rules apply to all. Together, these earn the community's trust.


SLIDE 12 — HOW THE THREE CONNECT
Let me connect my three topics. Eleven-point-one is our values — what we believe. Eleven-point-three is leading — how we act on those values. And eleven-point-four is governance — how we run the whole institution. Belief becomes behavior, and behavior becomes the system we govern by.


SLIDE 13 — KEY TAKEAWAYS
To summarize. 11.1 — values and ethics are honest, fair beliefs put into action. 11.3 — values-based leadership means leading by living those values openly. 11.4 — good governance means running the institution fairly and accountably. And the thread is: belief, to behavior, to system.


SLIDE 14 — THANK YOU
That concludes my report on Educational Leadership and Ethics — leadership grounded in values. Thank you, and I welcome your questions.
